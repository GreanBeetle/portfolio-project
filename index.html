About

"Do not worry. You have always written before and you will write now. All you have to do is write one true sentence. Write the truest sentence that you know."

Ernest Hemingway

As an adult, that old Hemingway quote has seen me through many a day. I'm John, 37, Portland-native, and someone who identifies on Twitter as a runner, reader, writer, and cinephile. The path leading from college to Epicodus has been long and checkered, but the one thing that has held true is writing. I love it. I have worked as a paralegal, a construction worker, a cook, a mail carrier, a mental health tech at a psychiatric hospital, and for many years, as a caregiver caring for my autistic cousin, Jack. During all those jobs and all that time, I wrote. Fiction, mostly. I've completed three full-length novels and numerous short stories. One of the books, Bloodwood, is self-published. Feel free to check it out on Goodreads here. Another of my manuscripts, The Oddclock, a beautiful, six-hundred-page tome, was finished in 2016 and lies on my dresser awaiting drastic revision. One of these months I will get around to changing and publishing it. For now, though, I am dedicating myself to the exhilarating, non-stop world of programming and Epicodus. It is a world to which I am warming up. It is full of possibility, and it is bright.

A few other things about me:

  1. I graduated from Portland State with a degree in political science.
  2. I play acoustic guitar, an old Yahama I inherited from my mom.
  3. One of my favorite movies is In Bruges . One of my favorite books is The Bone Clocks .
